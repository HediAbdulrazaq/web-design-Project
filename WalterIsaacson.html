Walter Isaacson

Walter Isaacson is an American writer, biographer, journalist, and professor known for his in-depth and insightful biographies of notable figures in history and science. Born on May 20, 1952, in New Orleans, Louisiana, Isaacson has had a prolific career in both journalism and writing. Isaacson studied at Harvard University, where he graduated magna cum laude in 1974. He then attended Pembroke College, Oxford, as a Rhodes Scholar, earning a degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics. His career in journalism began at The Sunday Times of London, followed by a position at Time magazine, where he eventually became the magazine's assistant managing editor. Isaacson's journalism experience honed his storytelling skills and provided a foundation for his future work as a biographer. Isaacson gained widespread recognition with his 1992 biography, "Kissinger: A Biography," which delved into the life and political career of Henry Kissinger, the former U.S. Secretary of State and National Security Advisor. This biography established Isaacson as a meticulous and thoughtful biographer, capable of capturing the complexities of influential figures. He went on to write several other acclaimed biographies, including "Benjamin Franklin: An American Life" (2003), "Einstein: His Life and Universe" (2007), and "Steve Jobs" (2011).
